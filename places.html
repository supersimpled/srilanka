Best Time To Visit Sri Lanka



Sri Lanka doesn’t experience extremes when it comes to weather conditions. The best time to visit Sri Lanka depends upon the region you’re keen to visit.

December to March is the perfect time for visiting most of the country (Highlands, West Coast, and South coast).

May to September is ideal for visiting the Eastern Coasts.

If you want to make a trip abroad and benefit from the exchange rate, then book your trip to Sri Lanka. So pamper yourself and indulge your senses in the rich heritage and diverse beauty by visiting these top Sri Lanka tourist places.

Frequently Asked Questions About Sri Lankan Tourist Places

How safe is Sri Lanka from the Coronavirus epidemic?

The Sri Lankan government and the tourist department are taking strict measures to curb the situation related to the Coronavirus outbreak. Visa-on-arrival has been prohibited to tourists from China and screening tests are in effect at the international airports.

What are the most beautiful places in Sri Lanka?

There are various places in the country of Sri Lanka that are considered to be quite beautiful. Some of them are:

  • Polonnaruwa
  • Yala National Park
  • Temple of the Tooth Relic
  • Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage
  • Adams Peak
  • Nuwara Eliya
  • Beira Lake

    The best Buddhist places in Sri Lanka are Mirisawetiya Stupa, Gangaramaya Buddhist Temple, and Kelaniya Raja Maha Vihara.







    Cultural Heritage:

    Sri Lanka's cultural heritage is a fascinating mix of different religions and ethnic groups, including Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity. The country is home to numerous ancient temples, ruins, and other cultural landmarks that reflect its diverse heritage. From the ancient city of Anuradhapura to the Temple of the Tooth in Kandy, Sri Lanka's cultural significance is truly awe-inspiring.

    Natural Beauty:

    Sri Lanka is blessed with breathtaking natural beauty, from its pristine beaches and turquoise waters to its lush rainforests and mountain ranges. The country is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including elephants, leopards, and hundreds of species of birds. Visitors can enjoy hiking, wildlife safaris, beach activities, and much more, making Sri Lanka an ideal destination for nature lovers.

    Cuisine:

    Sri Lankan cuisine is a delicious blend of spices, flavors, and ingredients that reflect the country's cultural diversity. From rice and curry to seafood specialties, visitors can sample a variety of dishes that tantalize the taste buds. Sri Lanka's tea culture is also renowned worldwide, and visitors can enjoy a cup of freshly brewed tea at one of the country's many tea plantations.

    Adventure Activities:

    Sri Lanka offers a range of adventure activities for visitors, including surfing, scuba diving, white-water rafting, and hiking. Visitors can explore ancient ruins, climb towering mountains, or take a scenic train ride through the tea country.

    Warm Hospitality:

    Sri Lanka is known for its warm hospitality and friendly people. Visitors can experience the local culture firsthand by staying in traditional homestays, visiting local markets, or participating in cultural events.



    In conclusion, Sri Lanka is a country of diverse beauty and cultural significance, with something to offer every visitor. Its rich history, stunning natural scenery, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality make it a unique and special destination.
